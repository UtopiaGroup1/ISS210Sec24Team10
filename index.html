Climate Change Vs Global Warming

Global Warming and Climate Change are terms that are used interchangeably, but when talking about Climate Change, it's important to understand that there is a difference between the two. Global Warming: It's a rise in global temperature because of the release of heat-trapping gases. When we burn oil, coal, or natural gases, Carbon dioxide is released which creates a warming effect. Climate Change is more of a general term used to describe multiple factors of climate factors leading to Climate Change. These factors can be seen locally or globally. A good way to look at it is, the changes in climate is happening in different ways because of their response to global warming. Examples would be the Austrialia wildfires, increase of ocean water levels, and the increase of harsh weather events.

Effects of Climate Change

Although the impacts of climate change aren't always the most obvious, over the last decade; almost every category of measurment for climate has worsened. Hotter global temperatures along with more servere storms have caused our earth to experience seasonal conditions unlike ever seen before. Droughts in already arid areas like California have caused not only humans, but also animals to adapt to saving water or finding alternative ways to source their needed water. If conditions don't imporve soon, these worsening conditions, will only be the beginning of a long road of worsening conditions globally.

Greenpeace

Greenpeace is "a network of independent organisations, which uses peaceful, creative confrontation to expose global environmental problems, and develop solutions for a green and peaceful future." Their goal is to ensure the ability of the earth to nurture life in all its diveristy by protecting biodiversity in all its forms, prevent pollution and abuse of the earth's ocean, land, air, and fresh water, end all nuclear threats, and to promote peace, global disarmament and non-violence. Greenpeace does not accept money from governments, corporations, or political parties. Inidividual contributions and foundation grants are there only source of funding. What is truly exceptional about Greenpeace is that they take accountabilty for their actions and have it stated in their core values sections saying "We take action based on conscience. This means we are accountable for our actions and take personal responsibility. We are committed to peacefulness; everyone on a Greenpeace action is trained in nonviolence." For more information visit their website at https://www.greenpeace.org/international/
